Loma Ranchito, Panama

Getting There

Your Journey

How to Reach Loma Ranchito

Loma Ranchito is located in the highlands of Herrera Province on Panama's Azuero Peninsula. The nearest major town is Chitré, the provincial capital, which serves as the main transport hub for the region. From Chitré, local roads lead up into the mountain communities including Loma Ranchito.

By Bus

The most affordable option. Direct buses depart from Panama City's Albrook terminal to Chitré every hour, operated by Transporte INAZUN and T.U.A.S.A. The journey takes approximately 4 hours and costs around $9. From Chitré, local transport connects to surrounding communities.

By Car

Driving from Panama City takes approximately 3.5 hours via the Pan-American Highway (Interamericana). Head west toward Santiago, then south at Divisa toward Chitré. Car rental is available in Chitré through Hertz and Thrifty, and a private vehicle gives you the freedom to explore the highland roads at your own pace.

By Air

Air Panama operates flights from Albrook Marcos A. Gelabert Airport in Panama City to Chitré's Alonso Valderrama Airport approximately 5 times per week. The flight takes under an hour. From the airport, taxis and local transport are available to reach the surrounding mountain communities.

Location & Coordinates

Loma Ranchito, Herrera

Loma Ranchito sits in the highlands of Herrera Province, Panama's smallest province, located on the Azuero Peninsula along the Pacific coast. The community is inland, elevated above the coastal plains, offering cooler temperatures and expansive views.

Chitré, the provincial capital, is the nearest major service centre — with hotels, restaurants, banks, a hospital, and bus connections to the rest of Panama.

Practical Tips

Herrera has two seasons — dry (December to April) and wet (May to November). The dry season is ideal for travel and coincides with most major festivals.
Panama uses the US dollar (Balboa). Cash is recommended for smaller communities; ATMs are available in Chitré.
Mobile coverage is generally available in Herrera Province. Major providers including Claro and Movistar serve the region.
Temperatures in the highlands are pleasantly mild — typically 24–28°C during the wet season and slightly warmer during the dry months.
Spanish is the language of the region. Locals are known throughout Panama for their warmth and hospitality — Herrera is affectionately called "the land where no one is a stranger."
